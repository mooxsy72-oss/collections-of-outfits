OUTFIT: an outfit belongs to the folk tradition of the indigenous peoples of the Russian Far North and Siberia, specifically styled after a women's traditional swing coat (such as a Nenets yagushka or Khanty sakh). This classification is supported by the heavy, tiered white fur borders, the integrated oversized hood, and the characteristic geometric mosaic borders lining the closures, cuffs, and tiers.The color palette is dominated by taupe-gray fabric, with pure white serving as the secondary color via extensive fur trim, highlighted by rust-brown, ochre, and white pattern work.The heavy, full-length coat features a flared A-line silhouette with a front-opening, non-waisted construction. Thick, plush white fox-style faux fur forms an oversized hood that blends into a dramatic shawl collar, continuing down the full length of the front plackets. The long, wide sleeves terminate in massive, tiered white fur cuffs.The lower half of the coat is constructed with two distinct, voluminous tiered panels, each completely bordered along the bottom edge with a deep band of matching plush white fur. Flanking every fur edge—along the center vertical opening, around the cuffs, and above the hem flounces—are applied decorative bands. These bands feature a dense, geometric zig-zag and interlocking block pattern executed in a mosaic style.Simple, flat black leather or suede pull-on winter boots support the coat.
