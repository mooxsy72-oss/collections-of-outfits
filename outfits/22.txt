OUTFIT: This ensemble belongs to the traditional Russian boyar or noble costume from the 16th–17th centuries. This classification is supported by the floor-length, A-line sarafan or opashen, the distinct circular barmas collar, the dramatic melon-shaped rukava sleeves, and the rigid, crown-like kokoshnik headpiece.The color palette is dominated by an ivory and olive-green base, accented with a vibrant secondary palette of crimson, ruby-red, and gold.The primary garment is a floor-length, structured dress with a flared silhouette. The body is made from a dense ivory brocade woven with a large-scale, repeating pattern of crimson and olive floral motifs. A wide, vertical decorative band splits the front centerline, featuring rows of white pearls framing large, raised ruby-red oval gems. At the high neckline sits a wide, circular barmas collar covered in dense gold filigree embroidery, red jewels, and a trim of dark brown mink fur.Beneath this, a white silk shirt features semi-sheer, voluminous balloon sleeves that gather tightly below the elbow into long, stiff forearms. These cuffs match the collar's dense green-and-gold embroidery and red jewels. A multi-stranded white pearl necklace rests flat against the chest.An olive-green velvet cape attaches at the shoulders, draping straight to the ground. The headwear consists of a white textile hood covered by a rigid, cylindrical kokoshnik encrusted with matching gold embroidery and teardrop gems.
