OUTFIT: This outfit belongs to the boyar and noble traditional Russian clothing tradition of the pre-Petrine era. The classification is supported by the multi-layered construction, a heavy fur-lined winter coat, a high ornamental headpiece, and intricate metallic trim.The overall color distribution features vibrant red as the dominant color, accented by secondary tones of warm terracotta, cream, and antique gold. Headpiece (Kokoshnik / Koruna): A rigid, crescent-shaped open crown constructed from structured red fabric. The surface is densely embellished with small pearls, red gemstones, and gold filigree embroidery in a symmetrical floral motif.Headscarf (Platok / Shavl): A voluminous, translucent shawl made from fine cream-colored mohair or wool. It features an airy, open-knit texture and draping loosely over the crown and shoulders.Outer Coat (Shuba / Opashon): A floor-length, heavy terracotta brocade winter coat with a relaxed, straight silhouette. It features wide, open sleeves and a plush brown fur collar that extends vertically down the entire front opening. The exterior fabric is detailed with a medium-density damask pattern. A wide gold braid trim with repeating geometric medallions finishes the hem and sleeve cuffs.Under-Gown (Sarafan): A structured, floor-length red gown visible beneath the coat. The central front seam is accented by a dense vertical band of dense gold lace embroidery and filigree trims.Shirt (Rubakha): A bright red silk base layer with loose, voluminous sleeves that bunch at the forearms. The cuffs are wide, stiff, and heavily encrusted with gold embroidery and pearls to match the headpiece.
