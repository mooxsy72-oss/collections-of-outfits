OUTFIT: ensemble embodies a high-fantasy interpretation of 18th-century European court attire, heavily leaning on Rococo and Justaucorps silhouettes. The dominant color is a deep, solid black, distributed across the tailored outerwear, vest, and trousers, while sharp contrast comes from a crisp white underlayer and opulent accents of metallic gold embroidery. The outfit relies on highly structured, stiff fabrics to maintain its sharp lines, with dense gold filigree floral patterning drawing the most visual attention against the dark background.The base layer consists of a lightweight white cotton or silk blouse, worn open at the neck with a deeply plunging V-shaped opening and a high, unstructured standing collar. The blouse's long sleeves terminate in voluminous, gathered white ruffles that extend past the wrists. Layered over this is a stiff, highly structured vest made from black brocade fabric, adorned with intricate, small-scale gold floral embroidery. The vest features a pointed front hemline, a low V-neckline, and a close-fitting waist construction.Matching black trousers feature a slim, tapered silhouette that tucks smoothly into the footwear. They are decorated with vertical bands of dense gold filigree embroidery running down the outer seams of the thighs.The outerwear is a structured knee-length frock coat, or justaucorps, in the same black brocade with all-over gold floral patterning. It features a stiff, tall mandarin collar, padded structured shoulders, and long sleeves that flip back into wide, dramatic turned-up cuffs lined with ornate gold trim. The coat is tailored close to the torso and flares outward from the waist into a stiff, structured skirt.Footwear consists of knee-high, form-fitting riding boots made of smooth, semi-glossy black leather with a subtle almond toe and low heels. A small, decorative gold chain accent hangs loosely from the right hip of the trouser waistline.
