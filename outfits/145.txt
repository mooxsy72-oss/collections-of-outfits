Visual attention focuses heavily on the ornate, jewel-encrusted collar and the towering, embellished headdress.The outer garment is a floor-length, A-line gown crafted from a heavy, structured white silk damask fabric featuring a subtle, medium-scale monochromatic floral jacquard pattern. It has a fitted upper bodice that opens at a wide, circular neckline to display the detached collar accessory. The defining feature is the pair of exaggerated, oversized hanging sleeves. These slit sleeves originate at the shoulder, falling in dramatic, sweeping points past the knees with scalloped lace-like gold edges, while the inner lining reveals a contrast of un-patterned white silk.Beneath the outer sleeves emerges a secondary layer of long, voluminous undersleeves made from an ice-blue brocade fabric with a gold vine pattern. These undersleeves are tightly gathered at the wrists with wide, matching bronze and gold embroidered cuffs encrusted with small pearls.The visual volume is created by the stiff, structured drape of the damask skirt and the layered, contrasting sleeve geometry.
