OUTFIT: a costume belongs to the traditional Russian court dress tradition, specifically a stylized interpretation of a 17th-century noblewoman's festive ensemble. This classification is supported by the tall, fan-shaped kokoshnik headpiece with a pearl mesh brim (podniz), the high-necked dushegreya or over-robe design, and the prominent vertical center-front decorative band (razgulyai).The color palette is strictly dual-toned, dominated by deep black velvet with antique gold serving as the sole secondary color for all patterns and ornamentation.The main garment is a floor-length, non-waisted gown with a structured A-line silhouette. It is made of black velvet, heavily adorned with a large-scale, repeating jacquard or embroidered pattern of gold baroque filigree, damask curls, and fleur-de-lis motifs densely covering the fabric. A wide, continuous gold-embroidered band splits the center front vertically from neckline to hem, pinned with a row of small, round gold buttons. The gown features a high, stiff mandarin-style collar covered in dense gold lace embroidery. The long sleeves are loosely gathered at the shoulder, tapering slightly toward the wrists, where they terminate in stiff, wide gold-embroidered cuffs.The towering, crescent-shaped kokoshnik is wrapped in matching gold metallic thread work, encrusted with small pearls and dark teardrop-shaped jewels. A delicate net of white seed pearls hangs over the upper forehead, complemented by long strands of pearl beads dangling from the sides of the headdress.
