OUTFIT: a Venetian Carnival masquerade ensemble blends Harlequin and Commedia dell'arte traditions. The silhouette is defined by an exaggerated, voluminous lower body and a highly structured, rigid torso.The central garment is a stiff, boned corset or bodice with a deeply pointed Basque waistline. It features a diamond-shaped Harlequin pattern in muted, multi-colored pastel tones, outlined with metallic gold braided trim. Beneath it sits a lightweight, white cotton or linen chemise. This chemise creates an oversized, multi-layered Elizabethan ruff collar at the scoop neckline and transitions into long, voluminous bishop sleeves. The sleeves are gathered tightly into segments with red satin ribbon bows, finishing in ruffled cuffs.The lower half consists of extremely puffy, oversized pumpkin shorts or bloomers made of structured white fabric that retains a globular shape, ending mid-thigh with delicate lace trim.The ensemble is dominated by a rigid, ornate filigree rabbit mask in cream and gold, complete with elongated, upright plush rabbit ears adorned with small gold bells. The wearer holds a black and gold jester staff topped with a miniature, masked jester head wearing a bi-corn hat. On the feet are pumps with a stable “glass” or “onion” heel in the Baroque style, made of thick textile or suede in colors that match the outfit.
