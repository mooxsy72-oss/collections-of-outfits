OUTFIT: outfit belongs to the 18th-century Western European court fashion tradition, heavily inflected with fantasy elements. The historical classification is supported by the highly structured Robe à la Française silhouette, featuring a low square neckline, stomacher, tight torso, and wide pannier-supported skirt. The fantasy elements are evident in the organic, overgrown floral application. The dominant color is deep forest green, appearing in the solid taffeta of the bodice and overskirt, while the secondary composition features a busy, romantic floral pattern in shades of dusty rose, mauve, and sage green on the underskirt panel and decorative trimmings.The primary garment is an ornate, full-body open gown. The tight, heavily boned bodice shapes a conical torso with a low, wide square neckline trimmed in delicate cream lace and small pink fabric flowers. It features three-quarter length fitted sleeves that terminate in voluminous, tiered engageantes made of layered, scalloped cream lace. The gown splits at the waist into a structured overskirt that drapes floor-length, supported by hidden undergarments to create side volume. Framing the open front of the skirt and trailing along the hem are dense, asymmetrical garlands of faux antiqued roses, dried leaves, and trailing vines. The visible underskirt panel is a heavy, stiff fabric featuring a large-scale, dense botanical and floral pattern in muted pink and green tones.A matching ornate metallic pendant necklace sits against the exposed upper chest, featuring intricate filigree work and a central gemstone.
