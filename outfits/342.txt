This classification is supported by the iconic, highly structured kokoshnik headdress, heavy silver ornamentation, and a silhouette evoking traditional Russian court dress infused with macabre elements. The overall color composition is monochromatic, dominated by a cold, frosty silver-blue and ice-grey, accented by metallic silver and white embroidery. Visual dominance is shared between the towering, circular headdress and the immense, pooling mermaid-style train fanning across the ground.The primary garment is a highly structured, full-body mermaid gown made from a mid-weight, semi-rigid satin or brocade fabric that provides strong shape retention. The upper bodice features a high, mock turtleneck collar and separate, dramatic leg-of-mutton sleeves that boast exaggerated, puffy padding at the shoulders before tapering tightly down the forearms to the wrists. Intricate, dense silver filigree embroidery and raised lace floral patterns coat the collar, shoulders, and chest panels, offering full bodily coverage. The gown fits skintight through the waist and hips before sharply flaring out at the knees into an expansive, circular train that puddles heavily into fluid folds on the floor, finished with a borders of silver winter-themed embroidery along the trailing hemline. Footwear is completely concealed beneath the voluminous skirt layers.The primary accessory is a large, halo-like kokoshnik headdress wrapped in dark fabric at the base and crowned by a massive, rigid semi-circular panel. This silver frame is heavily encrusted with metallic beadwork, pearls, and crystals, finished with long, dangling strands of silver beaded fringes that drape over the shoulders. Multiple layers of delicate, layered silver chain necklaces rest over the chest.
