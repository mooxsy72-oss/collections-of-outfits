OUTFIT: This ensemble draws heavily from traditional Mongolian and broader Central Asian nomadic attire, characterized by protective winter layering, heavy furs, and distinctive silver-and-turquoise ornamentation. The dominant color is dark charcoal-black, while secondary tones include earthy brown furs, vibrant turquoise accents, and pops of coral red. The primary garment is a thick, ankle-length wrap robe or deel, featuring a structured, high-standing collar trimmed with thin silver embroidery and a side-fastening front. The robe has wide, voluminous long sleeves that terminate in oversized, bulky fur cuffs. The silhouette is tightly cinched at the natural waist by a wide leather belt, which creates a flared, heavy drape in the lower skirt portion. The robe is constructed from a heavy, stiff wool or brocade fabric that retains its shape, with embroidered patterned ribbon trimming the cuffs and hems. A matching vest layer with a prominent, thick brown fur collar wraps around the shoulders, adding immense upper-body volume and texture. Accessories serve as major visual focal points. A large, structured cylindrical hat made of long-pile brown fur sits high on the head, adorned with a large central silver medallion inset with an oval turquoise stone and dangling metallic fringe. Around the neck hangs an oversized, heavy silver bib necklace featuring intricate filigree work, a prominent circular central turquoise stone, and a row of vertical silver drop pendants. The wide waist belt is decorated with a massive matching silver-and-turquoise medallion plate, from which detailed leather straps and silver roundels suspend down the side. A matching wide silver cuff bracelet with inset turquoise clusters is worn on the left wrist.
The hair is split into two long, highly structured, and voluminous three-strand braids that drape forward over the chest, secured at the ends with simple blue bands. These thick braids visually frame the heavy metallic jewelry and the face.
