OUTFIT: monochromatic ensemble draws from traditional Japanese martial attire, blending elements of the samurai and shinobi with fantasy wanderer themes. It is defined by its strict charcoal-black color palette, extensive fabric layering, and high-coverage silhouette, with the wide-brimmed conical hat and textured gauntlets drawing immediate focus.The primary layer consists of a textured linen kosode or wrap robe featuring a classic cross-neckline and loose, mid-length sleeves. Layered over this is a sleeveless, vest-like outer robe that splits at the sides, extending into tattered, asymmetrical hems that trail near the ankles. The lower body is covered by oversized, highly voluminous hakama style trousers that gather heavily at the shins. All garments are crafted from lightweight, coarsely woven matte linen or cotton with a heavily wrinkled, worn texture that pools and drapes loosely, completely obscuring the form.The waist is bound by a thick, multi-wrapped fabric sash of the same charcoal material, which tightly secures the fluid layers and supports tucked weaponry. The head and face are fully concealed by a continuous, densely wrapped fabric scarf covering the neck and lower face, topped by a rigid, low-profile conical jingasa style hat featuring a textured, weave-patterned dark finish. The forearms are wrapped in detailed leather gauntlets embellished with straps, metallic rivets, and integrated fingerless gloves. The lower legs are encased in tall, rugged black fabric or leather shin guards featuring a dense lace-up front closure.
