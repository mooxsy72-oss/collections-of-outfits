OUTFIT: a traditional ensemble belongs to the Russian folk clothing tradition, specifically featuring a prominent kokoshnik, a sarafan dress layered over a rubakha shirt, an apron, and bast shoes. The color palette is dominated by earthy cream, beige, and deep red, accented with highly dense, multicolor geometric and floral tapestry bands.The primary layer is a long, floor-length sarafan with a voluminous, slightly structured full skirt made from a heavy, densely patterned dark fabric with golden-red motifs. Layered over it is a mid-calf length, light-cream linen apron, gathered at the waist with an intricately woven, long red and gold sash that hangs down the front with tasseled ends. The apron hem is finished with horizontal bands of red embroidery and a wide strip of white pointed lace. Beneath these layers is a loose, voluminous white rubakha shirt with wide, gathered peasant sleeves, solid red shoulder panels, and cuffs trimmed in patterned bands.The most visually dominant accessory is a large, crescent-shaped, fan-like kokoshnik headdress covered in gold-and-red floral embroidery, featuring a delicate pearl net across the forehead and hanging pearl strands along the sides. Footwear consists of woven tan bast shoes worn over white leg wraps.
