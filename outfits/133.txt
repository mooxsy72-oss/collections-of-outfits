OUTFIT: ensemble belongs to a Slavic or Eastern European folk clothing tradition, resembling historical Slavic peasant or warrior winter attire. The classification is supported by the embroidered tunic reminiscent of a rubakha, a heavy sheepskin-style kozhukh coat, a woven textile sash, and rugged, strapped leather boots.The visual composition is defined by neutral, earthy tones, with beige and cream dominating the upper body and dark, weathered brown anchoring the lower half. Visual attention is drawn to the bulky, long-haired tan fur lining the coat's lapels and cuffs, and the dense red and black geometric cross-stitch embroidery framing the shirt’s central placket.The outermost layer is a heavy, knee-length, loosely structured coat made of textured cream canvas or linen fabric. It features a wide, open-front design with exaggerated, highly voluminous tan fur trim outlining the entire collar, front opening, and sleeve cuffs. Beneath it sits a lightweight, relaxed-fit cream linen tunic. This shirt has a short standing collar and an unbuttoned V-neck, densely adorned with a rectangular block of red and dark brown folk embroidery. The tunic’s asymmetrical hem gathers softly over the waist.A thick, patterned woven fabric sash in dark brown and muted red wraps multiple times around the waist, with long, fringed ends hanging down the front. The trousers are oversized, baggy, weathered dark brown leather or heavy wool pants that drape loosely into the footwear. The mid-calf boots are made of rugged, distressed brown leather, wrapped with multiple horizontal structural leather straps and metallic buckles around the shins.
