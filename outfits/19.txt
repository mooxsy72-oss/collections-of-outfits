OUTFIT: This outfit belongs to the outdated traditional Russian winter folk costume tradition. This classification is supported by the combination of a Pavlovo Posad-style floral headshawl, a flared wool semi-seasonal coat (resembling a ponitok or zipun), and a heavy patterned skirt.The color distribution features a dominant coarse gray-brown upper body, complemented by secondary deep red and black accents below and framing the face.Headwear: A bright red wool shawl patterned with dense, multicolor floral bouquets (yellow, blue, and white flowers). It is wrapped snugly around the head, framing the face, and tied securely under the chin with the remaining fabric draped over the chest.Outerwear (Zipun / Ponitok): A heavy, coarse-textured wool coat in a mottled gray-brown hue. It features a fitted upper torso, a wrap-style asymmetrical front closure, and straight, full-length sleeves. The waistline construction transitions into a flared, pleated A-line skirt section reaching just below the knees.Skirt: A full-length, voluminous gathered skirt made of dense burgundy fabric, decorated with a small, highly dense geometric print. Two horizontal black satin ribbons run parallel along the lower hem as a decorative trim.Gloves: Thick knit wool mittens featuring a dark navy and gray geometric folk motif across the exterior surface.
