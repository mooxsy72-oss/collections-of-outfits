OUTFIT: a traditional Russian folk winter ensemble, characterized by a heavy Pavlovo Posad style shawl, a fur-trimmed shearling coat (dushegreya or shuba variant), and an embroidered sarafan skirt. The dominant colors are deep indigo blue, burgundy, and earthy brown, accented by cream-white fur and intricate metallic gold embroidery.The silhouette is defined by voluminous, structured, and heavy layers designed for cold weather, covering the entire body down to the feet. A large, thick burgundy woolen shawl with a dense, colorful floral pattern and long fringe draping over the shoulders dominates the upper torso. Beneath it sits a hip-length, straight-cut dark brown shearling coat. This coat features prominent, thick cream-colored fur trim along the collarless front opening, cuffs, and bottom hem, adding substantial visual bulk.Layered underneath is a stiff, heavy indigo-blue wool skirt that flares slightly toward the ankles. The skirt features elaborate, symmetrical gold metallic embroidery running down the center front split and in a wide band around the hemline. The fabric is dense and completely opaque, holding a structured A-line shape without draping. Sturdy black winter boots peek out from under the hem.
