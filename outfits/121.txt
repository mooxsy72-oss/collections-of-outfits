OUTFIT: ensemble is a monochrome, cream-and-white avant-garde bridal fantasy outfit characterized by high-volume textures, intricate lace overlays, and an oversized whimsical headpiece that draws immediate visual attention. The silhouette heavily channels a highly romanticized, theatrical interpretation of Late Victorian or Edwardian bridal wear blended with surrealist fantasy elements, supported by the high neckline, fitted lace sleeves, and a structured, voluminous full skirt.The main garment is an A-line bridal gown featuring a fitted bodice with an illusion high neckline made of delicate, sheer white netting. The long sleeves are slim-fitting, constructed from semi-sheer white floral lace that ends in flared, scalloped cuffs. A large, structured bow made of stiff white tulle or organza is tied at the throat, creating an oversized, voluminous focal point. The gown features a defined, natural waistline where the fabric gathers into a heavy, structured full skirt that drapes to the floor in dense folds. The skirt fabric is a thick, cream-colored satin or brocade embellished with large-scale, raised floral lace appliques distributed vertically down the panels.The headpiece is the most visually dominant accessory, featuring a wide lace mask or blindfold that wraps around the head, obscuring the upper face with dense ruffles and floral lace trims. Sprouting from the top of the headpiece are two highly structured, oversized plush rabbit ears made of cream velvet or suede, lined with a smoother satin fabric. The base of these elongated ears is heavily clustered with dimensional fabric rosettes, tattered tulle tufts, and dangling satin ribbons. A sheer white tulle veil is attached to the back of the headpiece, cascading down the wearer’s back in light, airy layers that puddle onto the floor alongside the skirt.
