OUTFIT: ensemble draws from traditional Slavic folk attire, closely resembling a variant of a peasant chemise dress or vyshyvanka-style tunic. This classification is supported by the relaxed linen construction, dropped shoulder seams, and dense geometric folk embroidery bands tracking the openings and extremities. The dominant color is an unbleached, off-white cream, accented by secondary earthy tones of terracotta orange, dark brown, and charcoal gray distributed through the textile trims and belt accessories.The primary garment is a full-length, loose-fitting tunic dress made from a lightweight, breathable woven linen or hemp fabric with a soft drape and minimal shape retention. It features a deep V-neckline framed entirely by a dense, geometric embroidered trim that extends vertically down the center bodice. The dropped shoulders lead into voluminous, billowy bishop sleeves that are tightly gathered into structured, embroidered cuffs at the wrists. Additional horizontal bands of matching geometric embroidery wrap around the upper arms.The fabric is gathered heavily at the natural waist by a wide, textured textile sash belt in shades of rust-orange and brown, featuring long, dangling ties that hang down the front. Below the waist, the skirt flows into a straight, softly gathered hemline adorned with a wide, horizontal band of complex folk patterning running parallel to the bottom edge. Dark brown, unstructured leather boots with a matte texture cover the feet and ankles, completing the full-coverage, rustic silhouette.
