OUTFIT: A monochrome, open, translucent cream-colored bohemian-style off-the-shoulder mini dress relies on an openwork construction. This dress features an off-the-shoulder neckline, which completely exposes the collarbone and upper chest. Additionally, due to the openwork crochet and mesh structure of the fabric, the dress is semi-sheer throughout—at the sleeves, waist, and skirt, where skin is visible through the weave of the threads. It features an intricate, mixed-scale lace texture with varying density throughout. A dramatic, horizontally layered ruffle anchors the straight, elasticized off-the-shoulder neckline. This dense knit base creates a crisp, fitted silhouette, which is then visually extended to the floor solely by the cascading threads of long fringe. This feature incorporates dense rows of vertical tassel fringe that create a distinct dimensional overhang and combine with areas of exposed skin. The bodice fits close to the torso, revealing a prominent, large-scale open-stitch floral motif centered on the front abdomen. This central design transitions into a grid-like mesh pattern across the waist and hips. The long, semi-sheer sleeves feature wide, flared bell cuffs that drop below the arms, finished with matching fine fringe trims. The base of the dress's bottom is a fitted mini hem that hugs the figure tightly around the hips and reaches mid-thigh, reminiscent of a short sheath skirt. The individual yarn strands extend to the floor length, creating a fluid, swaying movement. The dress combines structural volume around the upper chest and lower arms with a form-fitting central silhouette. It is styled as a standalone piece without visible footwear or wearable accessories.
