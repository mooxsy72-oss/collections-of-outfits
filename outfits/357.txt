OUTFIT: outfit belongs to the 18th-century European Rococo masquerade ball tradition, specifically interpreting a robe à la française. This classification is supported by the rigid, conical stomacher bodice, the dramatic hip volume created by underlying paniers (hoop petticoats), the split-front overskirt, and the Venetian-style masquerade mask. The overall visual composition projects an atmosphere of regal grandeur and whimsical romance, dominated by a light sky-blue silk damask and a crisp ivory underskirt, symmetrically accented with shimmering metallic gold and light blue floral patterns.The primary garment is an expansive, floor-length court gown. The upper bodice features a low-cut, square neckline that exposes the collarbones and upper chest, transitioning into a rigid, structured stomacher panel that creates a highly defined, cinched V-shape at the waist. The stomacher is heavily embellished with gold filigree lace trim along the edges, vertical gold lacing channels, and a small blue fabric bow at the center bust. The tight elbow-length sleeves leave the forearms exposed, terminating in dramatic, voluminous engagement ruffles composed of three tiered layers of gathered blue fabric and white lace. Below the waist, the gown features a split-front overskirt supported by side-hoops to create an extremely wide, flattened rectangular silhouette. The overskirt is adorned down both front openings with a vertical column of nine structured gold metallic fabric bows. It reveals a heavy white satin petticoat underskirt detailed with vertical columns of blue floral embroidery, finished with a deeply gathered, tiered horizontal ruffle at the circular hemline.Footwear is entirely hidden under the dress. The hair is styled in a high, voluminous 18th-century poufont pompadour silhouette composed of powdered rolls and tight ringlets pinned tightly at the crown, with loose curls cascading down the back of the neck. It is decorated on the upper right side with an arrangement of two soft pink fabric roses with green leaves and a long, sweeping white and sky-blue feather plume. Wearable accessories include separate, skintight sky-blue satin opera gloves that extend over the elbows. At the neck sits a multi-layered white pearl choker necklace supporting a large, central diamond pendant. In the left hand is an intricate, folding ivory lace hand fan, while the right hand holds a slender gold rod attached to an ornate, metallic gold masquerade stick-mask encrusted with delicate filigree scrollwork.
