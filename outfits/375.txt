OUTFIT: ensemble blends Victorian-era tailoring with Gothic and dark fantasy aesthetics, characterized by a highly structured torso and dramatic contrasts in volume. The dominant colors are deep charcoal black and dark burgundy red, with color distribution separating the rich red upper layer from the dark, linear lower-body garments. Visual attention is intensely focused on the sharply cinched waist created by the overlapping corset and vest combination.The base upper-body layer is a dark burgundy red blouse or poet shirt crafted from a lightweight, fluid woven fabric with a matte texture. It features a high, structured neck collar that wraps completely around the throat, accented at the front by a small black ribbon tied into a delicate bow. The long sleeves are voluminous and billowy, ballooning outward from the shoulders before gathering tightly into elongated, structured red cuffs at the wrists that finish with small fabric ruffles. Layered tightly over the shirt is a rigid, underbust undergarment or corset made of deep burgundy fabric with vertical structural boning channels, a pointed front hemline, and metallic front busk closures. Overlapping this corset is a cropped, sleeveless black vest featuring a deeply plunging V-neckline, sharp lapels, and an open front that frames the central red shirt and corset details.On the lower body, a pair of slim-fit, high-waisted charcoal black trousers feature a subtle pinstripe pattern running vertically down the legs. The trousers offer a structured, linear fit that elongates the leg line, tapering slightly down to the ankles.Footwear consists of dark grey or black leather ankle boots with a prominent pointed-toe geometry, a smooth matte texture, and a low stacked heel. A single metal ring is worn as a visible accessory on the right index finger.
