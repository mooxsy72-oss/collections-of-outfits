OUTFIT: attire belongs to a Slavic folk clothing tradition, closely resembling a traditional Ukrainian vyshyvanka tunic paired with a winter kozhukh sheepskin coat. The classification is supported by the specific geometric red and black cross-stitch embroidery style, the long shirt silhouette with a split neckline, and the heavy fur-trimmed outer layer.The visual composition is defined by a clean white and cream base on the upper body, grounded by a solid dark blue lower half. Visual attention is split between the thick, shaggy grey and brown fur lining the coat's front panels and cuffs, and the dense, geometric red embroidery detailing the tunic's chest and mid-sleeve bands.The outermost layer is a heavy, knee-length, straight-cut winter coat made of textured cream wool or suede. It features a completely open-front structure, framed by bulky, long-haired grey-and-tan fur that forms a wide shawl collar, runs down the inner trim, and wraps the turned-back cuffs. Beneath it lies a relaxed, long-line white linen tunic. The tunic features a small band collar with a split V-neckline secured by a thin white cord tie, a pointed block of red-and-black embroidery over the placket, a wide horizontal embroidered stripe wrapping the upper forearms, and a matching geometric band along its straight hemline.The lower half consists of loosely fitted, non-bulky dark blue or charcoal wool trousers tucked straight into the footwear. The boots are mid-calf length, made of weathered, slouchy brown suede or leather with crinkled textures around the shins, finished with flat, dark soles.
