OUTFIT: Full-length winter ensemble draws directly from traditional Russian folk costume, specifically resembling the folklore depiction of Snegurochka (the Snow Maiden). The look is dominated by a frosty light blue and white palette, defined by heavily structured layers, intricate silver embroidery, and plush white fur trim that creates a highly voluminous, triangular silhouette.The primary outer garment is a heavy, structured A-line winter coat (shuba) reaching the ankles. It features a split-front design with ornate vertical silver filigree panels and a high, stiffened collar. The wide, dramatically flared open sleeves reveal an under-layer of a smooth, white satin shirt with gathered wrists and embroidered cuffs. A matching sleeveless over-vest or split cape layer sits atop the coat, adding bulk to the shoulders and torso. Thick, fluffy white fur trims all major edges, including the wide sleeve openings, the coat hem, and a matching hand muff resting at the waist.The crowning accessory is a highly structured, fan-shaped kokoshnik headdress. It is rendered in light blue textile, heavily encrusted with silver beads, pearls, and metallic embroidery, featuring long, delicate beaded strands hanging down the sides. The outfit offers total body coverage, utilizing thick, heavy, shape-retaining fabrics like brocade and velvet to maintain its rigid, majestic form.
