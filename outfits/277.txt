OUTFIT: a high-fantasy, romantic, and gothic-inspired bridal or ball gown set featuring a structured corset bodice, a high-neck collar piece, detachable statement sleeves, and a voluminous floor-length skirt. The entire ensemble follows a uniform monochromatic palette of crisp white and ivory, dominated by an intricate, large-scale floral and foliage lace pattern that covers almost every surface.The silhouette is anchored by a heavily structured, form-fitting boned corset bodice that covers the torso down to a dramatic, pointed Basque waistline. The corset features visible vertical boning channels, a sweetheart neckline, and a strapless base that structures the upper torso. Layered over the chest and neck is a separate, semi-sheer high-neck collar piece made of fine lace. This neck piece forms a stiff, close-fitting mock-neck collar and extends downward into a triangular shape over the collarbones, leaving a peek-a-boo opening at the chest. The shoulders are completely exposed in an off-the-shoulder, cold-shoulder configuration.The sleeves are detached, extremely oversized, and balloon-like in volume, creating a dramatic contrast with the tight bodice. They gather heavily at the upper arm with a ruffled, pleated tulle cuff, expand into a massive, lightweight bell shape that drapes down past the hips, and taper tightly at the wrists into long, fitted lace cuffs. The fabric of the sleeves is a semi-sheer, lightweight lace that retains a soft, airy puff.The lower half of the outfit consists of an expansive, multi-layered A-line or ball gown skirt that starts at the low-V waist and cascades to the floor, pooling into a wide, dramatic train at the back. The outer layer is made of a medium-weight, scalloped-edge floral lace that drapes in deep, heavy folds, giving the skirt immense fluid movement and visual prominence. Beneath the lace layer, structured petticoats or tulle underskirts provide the necessary loft and fullness to support the heavy lace drapery. The outfit provides full coverage from the neck to the feet, with the exception of the exposed bare shoulders and upper arms.
