OUTFIT: An outfit draws inspiration from traditional Mongolian and East Asian nomadic attire, characterized by a structured high-collar robe, a wide sash, and a voluminous, heavy fur-trimmed outer layer. The ensemble is defined by a highly layered, oversized silhouette where a massive, bulky coat dominates the upper body, contrasting with a full, structured skirt below. The dominant colors are rich chocolate brown, rust-red, cream, and stark white, accented with intricate metallic gold patterns.Outer Coat: An oversized, heavy, and voluminous mid-length coat. It features a wide, thick, plush rust-brown fur collar that frames the chest and shoulders, transitioning into voluminous, puffy cream-and-grey patterned faux-fur or textured shearling sleeves and side panels. It creates an intentionally bulky, dramatic upper-body proportion.Inner Robe (Deel-style): A structured, stiff textile robe worn beneath the coat. It features a high, rigid, close-fitting silver-grey mandarin collar that fully covers the neck. The body of the robe is a rich chocolate brown midweight fabric with a subtle sheen, densely decorated with an all-over, medium-scale metallic gold brocade floral or cloud-scroll pattern.Waist Sash: A wide, flat, structured white fabric sash or cummerbund wraps tightly around the natural waist, creating a sharp division between the patterned bodice and the skirt.Skirt: A voluminous, full-length maxi skirt made of crisp, heavy white structural fabric that holds its flared shape. It features sparse, large-scale gold embroidered pictorial motifs near the hemline and drapes in deep, structured folds to the ground, providing complete lower-body coverage.Earrings: Intricate, oversized, and visually prominent shoulder-dusting statement earrings. They consist of a large, circular, metallic silver medallion base featuring traditional filigree or embossed motifs, from which numerous long, delicate strands of white seed pearls or beads cascade down past the collarbone, creating a heavy, dramatic fringe.
