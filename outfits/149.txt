OUTFIT: ensemble is a recreation of traditional Russian nobility attire (boyarynya) from the 16th or 17th century. This classification is supported by the heavy, elaborate layered robes, the prominent decorative collar, and the jewel-encrusted kokoshnik headdress. The color palette features a dominant rose-pink and gold combination, balanced by secondary cream and pearl tones distributed throughout the intricate textiles.The outer garment is a lavish opashen-style robe with a stiff, A-line silhouette that dramatically flares toward the floor. It features open, wide-cut, elongated hanging sleeves that cascade past the hands, exposing a lighter cream, full-sleeved undergarment gathered at the wrists. The heavy, brocade-like pink fabric maintains high shape retention, adorned with large-scale metallic gold damask patterns. Down the center-front opening runs a broad, structured placket densely embroidered with gold thread and small gems.Resting over the shoulders is a wide, circular barmy-style collar with a scalloped edge, meticulously decorated with gold filigree and pearls. The lower hem reveals a coordinating pink skirt panel with matching embroidered vertical bands.The crowning accessory is a crescent-shaped kokoshnik headdress covered in dense pearl embroidery, colorful gemstones, and fine gold beadwork, complete with long, delicate pearl strands hanging down past the shoulders. A heavy gold filigree chain necklace with a central pendant rests over the collar, completing the highly structured, high-coverage silhouette.
